The Edge · Origin

Built in the open.
Losing out loud.

You've seen too many crypto projects promise the moon, hide their results, and disappear when the curve goes down. This isn't that.

BTC Predictor was built with one constraint: every signal, every bet, every loss is committed on Polygon before execution. Not after. Not selectively. Before. The blockchain timestamp doesn't lie.

The current win rate is loading.... That's visible to anyone. Every losing bet is a data point that trains the next version. We don't hide the red months — we publish them as evidence that the system is real.

The bet is simple: markets are inefficient in predictable ways. With enough data, enough architecture, and enough honesty about what works and what doesn't — the edge emerges. We are in the data-collection phase. What you support is the research, not a guarantee.

Common Questions

You should be
skeptical.

The win rate is low. Why would I support this?
Because the win rate is published. That skepticism is exactly what this project was built for. On-chain, you verify — you don't trust. A system that hides losing months is the red flag, not the one that publishes them. We're in the data collection phase. Statistical significance requires hundreds of bets, not dozens.
What do I get in return?
A permanent on-chain record that you were here early. Founding tier contributors get access to raw model outputs. But more concretely: the on-chain record of who supported this first is immutable. You can't buy your way in retroactively.
Is my contribution safe? Can I verify it?
Yes. Your transaction goes directly to 0x7Ac896F18ce52a0520dA49C3129520f7B70d51f0 on Polygon. Verifiable on PolygonScan within seconds. There are no smart contracts between you and the wallet — it's a direct transfer. Always verify the address yourself before sending.
I don't have time to set this up right now.
The transaction takes 11 seconds on Polygon. You've already spent longer reading this page. If you have MetaMask installed, tap "Open in MetaMask" and it pre-fills everything.
1 POL is too small to matter, isn't it?
The first block in any chain is exactly that size. What matters is that it exists — recorded, timestamped, immutable. The on-chain record doesn't care about the amount. It cares that you were there.
